MUTUAL NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

In connection with the evaluation of a possible business relationship between the parties (the “Purpose”),
MetaLoop INC, on behalf of its affiliates (“MPCVault”), and Licensed Party (You) on behalf of its affiliates (“Company”, and together with MetaLoop, collectively the “Parties”, and each, a “Party”), agree to the following mutual non-disclosure terms (the “Agreement”):

1. Confidential Information. A Party (the “Recipient”) may have access to information from the other Party (the “Discloser”) which (a) relates to the Discloser’s technology, intellectual property, business plans, financial statements, internal management tools, and systems, research, product roadmap, pricing, customers, suppliers, or employees; or (b) has been identified as confidential or would be understood to be confidential by a reasonable person under the circumstances (“Confidential Information”). Notwithstanding the foregoing or any other provision of this Agreement, Discloser will not disclose (x) any information that would be a violation of the proprietary rights of any third party, or (y) information protected by the International Traffic in Arms Regulations or any matter that is export-controlled.

2. Use. The recipient may use Confidential Information only for the Purpose and shall protect Confidential Information, in the same manner, it protects the confidentiality of its own confidential information of similar nature, but in no event using less than a reasonable standard of care. The Recipient may disclose Confidential Information to its employees, affiliates, directors, subcontractors, and consultants (“Representatives”) who have a need to know and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those contained herein. The recipient will be responsible for the compliance of such Representatives with the terms of this Agreement. Recipient shall immediately give notice to Discloser of any unauthorized use or disclosure of Discloser’s Confidential Information. The recipient shall assist Discloser in remedying the unauthorized use or disclosure of Discloser’s Confidential Information. This Agreement imposes no obligation to proceed with any business transaction.

3. Exceptions. Nothing in this Agreement limits Recipient’s use of any information a. previously known to the Recipient without restriction before receipt from the Discloser; b. independently developed by the Recipient without the use of the Confidential Information; c. acquired by the Recipient from a third party which was not, to the Recipient’s knowledge, under an obligation not to disclose such information; d. publicly available through no breach of this Agreement; or e. approved in writing by the Discloser for disclosure. The Recipient may disclose Confidential Information to the extent it is compelled to do so by law or otherwise necessary to establish the rights of either Party under this Agreement and shall provide reasonable prior notice to the other Party of such disclosure to the extent legally permitted.

4. Ownership. Confidential Information disclosed under this Agreement shall remain the property of the Discloser. The Discloser does not grant any express or implied license or right to or under any intellectual property right in its Confidential Information under this Agreement. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to limit the Recipient’s right to independently develop information, technology, or products for itself or for others that may compete with the Discloser provided Recipient does not violate its confidentiality obligations herein. All Confidential Information is provided “AS IS” and without any warranty, express, implied or otherwise, regarding the Confidential Information’s completeness, accuracy or performance.

5. Term; Termination. The recipient’s duty to protect Confidential Information expires three (3) years from the date of disclosure. This Agreement may be terminated by either Party upon thirty (30) days written notice to the other Party; provided, however, the Recipient’s obligations with respect to any Confidential Information received hereunder shall continue for a period of three (3) years from the termination date of this Agreement. Upon the Discloser’s written request, the Recipient shall promptly return or destroy the Discloser’s Confidential Information; provided, however, the Recipient may retain a copy of such information, subject to the terms of this Agreement, to comply with its internal recordkeeping requirements or applicable law.

6. Remedies; Governing Law. Each Party agrees that a breach of this Agreement could result in irreparable harm and that each Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief in addition to any other rights and remedies at law it may have. This Agreement shall be construed in accordance with the laws of the State of California, without giving effect to conflicts of laws principles. Each Party consents to the exclusive jurisdiction and venue of the federal and state courts located in California.

7. Entire Agreement. This Agreement sets forth the entire understanding between the parties with respect to its subject matter, superseding any prior or contemporaneous agreements. Recipient shall not export any Confidential Information of the Discloser in violation of any United States export laws. Neither Party may assign its rights or delegate its duties or obligations under this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other Party. There are no third-party beneficiaries of this Agreement. This Agreement may not be modified except by a written instrument signed by both parties and failure to enforce any provision of this Agreement shall not constitute a waiver. In the event that a provision of this Agreement is held to be unenforceable, that provision shall be severed and the remainder of this Agreement shall remain in effect. This Agreement may be executed electronically and in two or more counterparts, each of which together shall constitute one instrument.

8. Effective Date. This Agreement shall be effective on the date of licensed party agrees to terms and policy of the MetaLoop or the date on which they first logged in to MPCVault (“Effective Date”).
